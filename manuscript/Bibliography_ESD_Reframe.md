# Annotated Bibliography: DOM Structural Diversity, Isomer-Resolved Analysis, and a Coverage-Based Diversity Framework

*Compiled for an ES&T Critical Review on DOM "clonome" reframing.*
*Date assembled: 2026-05.*

## Executive Summary (~200 words)

This bibliography supports a perspective that reframes dissolved organic matter (DOM) as an isomer-resolved "clonome" measurable with the same coverage-based, Hill-number machinery used in immunology and microbial ecology. The strongest 2024-2026 work clusters in: (i) **TIMS-FT-ICR-MS for DOM** (Ridgeway et al. 2024 JASMS at 21 T; ongoing OSA-TIMS), (ii) **LC-FT-ICR-MS for DOM** (Lechtenfeld et al. 2024 ES&T direct injection; Wagner et al. 2025 ES&T isomeric dispersity index), (iii) **cryogenic IR + ion mobility platforms** (Garimella et al. 2024 Anal Chem messenger-tagging IR-IMS), (iv) **single-molecule covalent nanopores** (Wang et al. 2025 Nat Commun aldehyde isomer detection), (v) **AI/foundation models** for spectral elucidation (NMR-Solver 2025/2026, SCARF/MIST-CF Goldman 2023), and (vi) **GNPS extensions** for environmental molecular networking. Foundational classics with the highest citation weight are Hertkorn et al. 2006 (CRAM), Koch & Dittmar 2006 (AI), Zark & Dittmar 2018 (universal structures), Hill 1973 + Chao 1984 + Chao & Jost 2012 (diversity theory), Wang et al. 2016 (GNPS), Smith & Kelleher 2013 (proteoform), and Jiao et al. 2010 (microbial carbon pump). **Gaps the perspective can fill:** (a) no DOM paper has yet imported coverage-based Hill-number rarefaction; (b) the DOM/proteoform/clonotype analogy is unclaimed; (c) isomer-resolved diversity from TIMS or cryo-IR has never been propagated into ecosystem-scale comparisons.

**Legend.** Most-cited classics marked **[C]**; very recent (2024-2026) marked **[*]**.

## Cluster 1 — DOM FT-ICR-MS Molecular Characterisation

> **Schramm, Kalinski, Arini, Da Silva & Petras (2026).** "Uncovering the Structural Space of Marine Dissolved Organic Matter." *Annu. Rev. Mar. Sci.* doi:10.1146/annurev-marine-040224-031331. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* Most current synthesis of structure-resolved DOM analytics; directly frames the audience and motivation for the perspective.

> **Wagner, Hertkorn, Lechtenfeld, et al. (2025).** "Functional Group Distribution Shapes Chemical Properties of Degraded Terrestrial and Marine Dissolved Organic Matter." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 59(49):26539-26549. doi:10.1021/acs.est.5c01998. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* Defines the isomeric dispersity index that operationalizes within-formula diversity — central machinery the perspective generalizes.

> **Boiteau, Dittmar et al. (2024).** "Relating Molecular Properties to the Persistence of Marine DOM with LC–Ultrahigh-Resolution MS." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* doi:10.1021/acs.est.3c08245. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* Links functional-group composition to persistence — provides the mechanism the diversity index must explain.

> **Petras et al. (2024).** "Enhancing Molecular Characterization of DOM by Integrative Direct Infusion and LC Nontargeted Workflows." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 58:11216-11226. doi:10.1021/acs.est.4c00876. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* Reference workflow integrating DI-FT-ICR with LC-MS/MS for environmental DOM annotation.

> **Patriarca, Hawkes et al. (2023).** "Marine DOM Shares Thousands of Molecular Formulae Yet Differs Structurally across Major Water Masses." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* doi:10.1021/acs.est.1c04566.
> *Relevance:* Empirical demonstration that identical formulae mask different isomer ensembles — the central paradox the perspective addresses.

> **Bahureksa, Borch, Aiken, McKenna, et al. (2021).** "Soil Organic Matter Characterization by FT-ICR MS: A Critical Review." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 55(14):9637-9656. doi:10.1021/acs.est.1c01135. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Authoritative review of FT-ICR sample handling, formula assignment, and diagnostics that inform any new diversity framework.

> **Hawkes et al. (2020).** "An international laboratory comparison of FT-ICR MS for DOM." *Limnol. Oceanogr. Methods* 18:235-258. doi:10.1002/lom3.10364.
> *Relevance:* Establishes reproducibility floor for cross-lab diversity comparisons.

> **Zark & Dittmar (2018).** "Universal molecular structures in natural dissolved organic matter." *Nat. Commun.* 9:3178. doi:10.1038/s41467-018-05665-9. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Demonstrated formula-level convergence across ecosystems — the very fact that motivates moving beyond formula counts.

> **Hertkorn, Harir, Koch, Michalke & Schmitt-Kopplin (2013).** "High-field NMR spectroscopy and FTICR mass spectrometry: powerful discovery tools for marine DOM." *Biogeosciences* 10:1583-1624. doi:10.5194/bg-10-1583-2013. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Quantitatively shows that each FT-ICR formula in DOM hides hundreds of chemical environments — direct empirical justification for isomer-aware diversity.

> **Hertkorn et al. (2008).** "Natural Organic Matter and the Event Horizon of Mass Spectrometry." *Anal. Chem.* 80(23):8908-8919. doi:10.1021/ac800464g. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Defines the structural ceiling of MS-only DOM analysis and the impossibility of full chemical annotation.

> **Marshall & Rodgers (2008).** "Petroleomics: Chemistry of the underworld." *Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA* 105(47):18090-18095. doi:10.1073/pnas.0805069105. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Foundational FT-ICR petroleomics framework whose isomer/dimensional logic carries directly to DOM.

> **Koch & Dittmar (2006).** "From mass to structure: an aromaticity index for high-resolution mass data of natural organic matter." *Rapid Commun. Mass Spectrom.* 20:926-932. doi:10.1002/rcm.2386 (corrigendum 2016, 30:250). **[C]**
> *Relevance:* The AI / AI_mod metric used in every modern DOM FT-ICR paper, including IOS / NOSC indices the perspective extends.

> **Hertkorn, Benner, Frommberger, Schmitt-Kopplin et al. (2006).** "Characterization of a major refractory component of marine organic matter (CRAM)." *Geochim. Cosmochim. Acta* 70:2990-3010. doi:10.1016/j.gca.2006.03.021. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* The CRAM concept anchoring DOM persistence discussions and serving as benchmark structural class for diversity claims.

## Cluster 2 — TIMS-FT-ICR-MS for DOM

> **Ridgeway, Khodjaniyazova, Garrett, Kew, Hendrickson, Fernandez-Lima (2024).** "Revisiting Dissolved Organic Matter Analysis Using High-Resolution Trapped Ion Mobility and FT-ICR Mass Spectrometry." *J. Am. Soc. Mass Spectrom.* 35(10):2400-2407. doi:10.1021/jasms.4c00232. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* The 21 T TIMS-FT-ICR Harney River benchmark — the analytical backbone that justifies isomer-resolved DOM diversity claims.

> **Leyva, Tose, Porter, Wolff, Jaffé & Fernandez-Lima (2019).** "Understanding the structural complexity of dissolved organic matter: isomeric diversity." *Faraday Discuss.* 218:431-440. doi:10.1039/C8FD00221E. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* First demonstration of ESI-TIMS-FT-ICR isomer counting (6-10 isomers per formula) on DOM — direct empirical seed for clonome reframing.

> **Leyva, Jaffé & Fernandez-Lima (2020).** "Structural Characterization of Dissolved Organic Matter at the Chemical Formula Level Using TIMS-FT-ICR MS/MS." *Anal. Chem.* 92:11960-11966. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.0c02347.
> *Relevance:* Adds MS/MS structural assignment to TIMS-resolved isomers — methodological cornerstone.

> **Leyva, Tose et al. (2022).** "Description of Dissolved Organic Matter Transformational Networks at the Molecular Level." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 57(2):1458-1468. doi:10.1021/acs.est.2c04715.
> *Relevance:* Uses CASI-CID FT-ICR MS/MS on multiple ecosystems; <10% structural overlap shows hidden divergence underlying formula similarity.

> **Leyva et al. (2022).** "Unsupervised Structural Classification of Dissolved Organic Matter Based on Fragmentation Pathways." *Environ. Sci. Technol.* 56(2):1458-1468. doi:10.1021/acs.est.1c04726.
> *Relevance:* Computational framework converting fragmentation patterns into structural families — a candidate clonotype operational definition.

> **Tose, Benigni, Leyva, Sundberg, Ramirez, Romero, Fernandez-Lima (2018).** "Coupling trapped ion mobility spectrometry to mass spectrometry: trapped ion mobility spectrometry-time-of-flight MS vs trapped ion mobility spectrometry-FT-ICR MS." *Rapid Commun. Mass Spectrom.* 32:1287-1295. doi:10.1002/rcm.8165.
> *Relevance:* TIMS coupling rationale that informs Ridgeway 2024 21 T setup.

> **Benigni & Fernandez-Lima (2016).** "Oversampling Selective Accumulation Trapped Ion Mobility Spectrometry Coupled to FT-ICR MS." *Anal. Chem.* 88(14):7404-7412. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.6b01946. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Defines OSA-TIMS, the high-resolving-power ion-mobility mode behind isomer counts in DOM.

## Cluster 3 — SLIM and Cyclic IMS for Complex Mixtures

> **Naylor, Garimella et al. (2025).** "Recent advances in high-resolution traveling wave-based ion mobility separations coupled to mass spectrometry." *Mass Spectrom. Rev.* doi:10.1002/mas.21902. **[*]**
> *Relevance:* Most current review summarizing SLIM + cyclic IMS performance envelopes that bound future DOM isomer experiments.

> **Norheim, Anderson, Garimella et al. (2024).** "Mobility Selective Ion Soft-Landing and Characterization Enabled Using Structures for Lossless Ion Manipulation (SLIM)." *Anal. Chem.* doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.3c05753 (PMC11191849). **[*]**
> *Relevance:* SLIM-based mobility-selective preparative MS — a route to validate clonome members by post-deposition characterization.

> **Giles, Ujma, Wildgoose, Pringle, Richardson, Langridge & Green (2019).** "A Cyclic Ion Mobility-Mass Spectrometry System." *Anal. Chem.* 91(13):8564-8573. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.9b01838. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* Defines Waters cIMS (R~750 at 100 passes) — the commercial alternative to SLIM that puts isomer separation within community reach.

> **Deng, Webb, Garimella, Smith et al. (2017).** "Ultra-High Resolution Ion Mobility Separations Utilizing Traveling Waves in a 13 m Serpentine Path Length Structures for Lossless Ion Manipulations Module." *Anal. Chem.* 89(8):4628-4634. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.7b00185.
> *Relevance:* The 13-m SLIM module — performance milestone routinely cited as the >R~1000 IMS standard.

> **Hamid, Ibrahim, Garimella, Smith et al. (2015).** "Characterization of Traveling Wave Ion Mobility Separations in Structures for Lossless Ion Manipulations." *Anal. Chem.* 87:11301-11308. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.5b02481.
> *Relevance:* Original SLIM TW characterization underpinning all later >100-m SUPER work.

> **Garimella, Ibrahim, Webb, Tolmachev, Anderson, Prost, Norheim, Baker, Smith (2014).** "Simulation of electric potentials and ion motion in planar electrode structures for lossless ion manipulations (SLIM)." *J. Am. Soc. Mass Spectrom.* 25:1890-1896. doi:10.1007/s13361-014-0976-y. **[C]**
> *Relevance:* The first SLIM paper — origin of the entire ultra-high-resolution IMS line.

## Cluster 5 — Two-Dimensional FT-ICR-MS

> **van Agthoven, Lam, O'Connor, Rolando, Delsuc (2019).** "Two-dimensional mass spectrometry: new perspectives for tandem mass spectrometry." *Eur. Biophys. J.* 48:213-229. doi:10.1007/s00249-019-01348-5.

> **Floris, Chiron, Lynch, Barrow, Delsuc, O'Connor (2018).** "Top-Down Deep Sequencing of Ubiquitin Using Two-Dimensional Mass Spectrometry." *Anal. Chem.* 90:15336-15343. doi:10.1021/acs.analchem.8b03113.
> *Relevance:* Demonstrates 2D FT-ICR proteoform sequencing — direct analog of the DOM 2D MS application path.

> **Floris, van Agthoven, Chiron, Soulby, Wootton, Lam, Barrow, Delsuc, O'Connor (2016).** "2D FT-ICR MS of Calmodulin: A Top-Down and Bottom-Up Approach." *J. Am. Soc. Mass Spectrom.* 27:1531-1538. doi:10.1007/s13361-016-1431-z.

> **van Agthoven, Chiron, Coutouly, Delsuc, Rolando (2011).** "Two-Dimensional ECD FT-ICR Mass Spectrometry of Peptides and Glycopeptides." *Anal. Chem.* 84:5589-5595. doi:10.1021/ac3004874.
> *Relevance:* Shows ECD as 2D-MS dimension, one of multiple dissociation modes invocable for DOM.

> **Wootton, Lam, van Agthoven et al. (2022).** "In Silico Demonstration of Two-Dimensional Mass Spectrometry Using Spatially Dependent Fragmentation." *J. Am. Soc. Mass Spectrom.* 33:2154-2162. doi:10.1021/jasms.2c00241.

## Cluster 6 — LC-FT-ICR-MS for DOM
